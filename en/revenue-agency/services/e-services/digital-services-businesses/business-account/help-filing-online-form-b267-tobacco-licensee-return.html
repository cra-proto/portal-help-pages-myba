Before you file

Use the webforms to file an original or amended B267 return. The webforms will let you:

  • create a new return
  • make changes to a return you have already filed
  • validate data in real time
  • print or save a PDF version of your confirmation page

Have your paperwork and other documents ready.

For security reasons, our servers end a B267 web forms session after 18 minutes of inactivity. If your session ends, you will have to reauthenticate yourself and re-enter your data.

How to file

Screen id: B-RD-B267-01

Inventory for tobacco products: Step 1 of 4

Total Production: Enter the total quantity, in units or kilograms, for each tobacco product produced.

Previous: Returns you to the previous screen.

Next: Takes you to the next step.

Note: The “Previous” and “Next” buttons will be displayed at the bottom of most screens and will work as described above.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-02

Inventory for tobacco products: Step 2 of 4

Packaged: Not stamped–to excise warehouse: Enter the total quantity, in units or kilograms, of packaged but not stamped cigarettes, tobacco sticks, manufactured tobacco, and cigars you placed in an excise warehouse.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-03

Inventory for tobacco products: Step 3 of 4

Tobacco products–partially manufactured tobacco: Enter the opening inventory and any additions, reductions, or adjustments to your partially manufactured tobacco inventory.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-04

Inventory for tobacco products: Step 4 of 4

Tobacco products–raw leaf tobacco: Enter the opening inventory and any additions, reductions or adjustments to your raw leaf tobacco inventory.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-05

Excise duty: Step 1 of 4

Manufactured in Canada

For cigarettes, tobacco sticks, other manufactured tobacco, cigars and raw leaf tobacco:

Enter the total quantity, in units, and the duty payable amount, in dollars, for each tobacco product that was stamped during the period.

Note for additional duty on cigars (rate per cigar) and additional duty on cigars (rate based on sale price):

An additional duty is imposed on cigars at the time of their delivery to a purchaser. The calculation is based either on the set rate per cigar or the set percentage computed on the sale price.

For each cigar, you must include on the form only the greater of the calculated amounts.

  • For additional duty on cigars (rate per cigar)

    In the “Units” field, enter the total number of stamped cigars that were delivered to purchasers during the period and on which the additional duty on cigars must be paid at the effective set rate per cigar.

    Calculate the amount of additional duty payable, by multiplying the number of cigars entered by the effective rate (set dollar amount) of additional duty for cigars. Enter the result in the “Duty payable” field only if the result is greater than the result calculated for additional duty on cigars (rate based on sales price).

  • For additional duty on cigars (rate based on sale price)

    In the “Units” field, enter the total number of stamped cigars that were delivered to purchasers during the period and on which the additional duty on cigars must be paid at the effective set percentage rate per cigar.

    Determine the combined total sale price of these cigars.

    Calculate the amount of additional duty payable by multiplying the combined total sale price of the cigars delivered to all purchasers by the effective rate (set percentage). Enter the result in the “Duty payable” field only if that result is greater than the result calculated for additional duty on cigars (rate per cigar).

Screen id: B-RD-B267-06

Excise duty: Step 2 of 4

Duty-free shops, ships stores, and customs bonded warehouses

Enter the total quantity, in units, and the duty payable amount, in dollars, for each tobacco product destined for duty-free shops, ships stores, or custom bonded warehouses that was stamped during the period.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-07

Excise duty: Step 3 of 4

Exports not exceeding 1.5% (special duty)

Enter the total quantity, in units, and the duty payable amount, in dollars, for each stamped tobacco product that was exported during the period in quantities that did not exceed 1.5% of your previous calendar year’s total production of cigarettes.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-08

Excise duty: Step 4 of 4

Exports exceeding 1.5% (special duty)

Enter the total quantity, in units, and the duty payable amount, in dollars, for each stamped tobacco product exported during the period, including quantities that exceeded 1.5% of your previous calendar year’s total production of the tobacco product.

Screen ID: B-RD-B267-STP

Stamp inventory

For each tobacco product, enter the opening inventory, as well as the number of stamps you received, stamps you used for products, destroyed stamps, and any stamp inventory adjustments.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-REV

Review

Check all of your data and make any changes before sending your return. If you find an error, select “Previous” to make corrections.

File a refund to offset an amount payable

If you would like to apply for a B256 Application for Refund with your return, select "Yes" to enter the details of the refund in the File a Refund webform.

Will you make the payment within the next five business days or by the payment due date?

Select “Yes” if you intend on making a payment.

Certification statement: You must tick the box certifying that the information you gave is accurate and complete before you can select “Submit.”

Submit: Automatically sends the return to the Canada Revenue Agency and will generate a confirmation page.

Screen id: B-RD-B267-CNF

Confirmation

The confirmation page gives details about your return information, along with the filing date and confirmation number of the return.

Keep the confirmation number for your records. Do not send us a paper copy of the documents you filed online.

Print/Save: Prints this page.
PDF Version: You can save and print a copy of this return in PDF format.
File another return: Returns you to the first page so you can file another return for the same account.

After you file

Confirmation number

You will receive a confirmation number after you have corrected all errors and sent your return for processing. This confirmation number means that the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) received your return.

Do not mail us a paper copy of the returns you filed online.

After we receive your return

We may contact you to verify your supporting documents. This does not mean you are being audited. It is simply part of our process for all paper and online returns.

If the CRA sends you a notice of assessment and you are registered to receive email notifications, the CRA will send you a notification that there is mail for you in My Business Account.

Email notifications

Most correspondence is only available online in My Business Account by default, except when a business has changed its delivery method to receive paper mail. Business owners registered in My Business Account can add an email address to receive notifications when their Canada Revenue Agency mail is available to view in their secure online account, and when other important changes are made to their account. To register, select “Profile” then “Manage notification preferences” and follow the steps.

Making a payment

If you have a balance owing, you can make your payment in many different ways:

  • the "Proceed to pay" button on the Confirmation screen
  • your financial institution's online banking or telephone banking services
  • online using the CRA My payment service
  • set up a pre-authorized debit agreement using My Business Account
  • in person at your financial institution in Canada with a remittance voucher

If you need a remittance voucher, call 1-800-959-5525.

Note: Financial institutions will not accept photocopied remittance vouchers.

For information on how to make your payment, go to Make a payment to the Canada Revenue Agency. If you send your payment late, you might have to pay penalties and interest at the prescribed rate.

Troubleshooting B267 online returns

Why can't you change your browser setting while you are in a secure session?

Do not modify your browser setting while in a secure B267 webforms session because it may cause your browser to freeze or the session to end.

The secure web pages for the B267 return do not display properly. Why?

Several factors can affect how these pages will display on your system. Try the following:

  • use the default browser settings
  • maximize your browser window
  • close the favorites window
  • use the browser's reload or refresh button
  • make sure you have enough RAM for your browser's requirements

You were away from your computer for 30 minutes. Can you still complete your return?

For security reasons, our servers will end a B267 webforms session after 18 minutes of inactivity. You will have to reauthenticate yourself and re-enter your data because any data you entered will have been lost.

You lost the connection to the B267 webforms secure site. What should you do?

You will have to reauthenticate yourself and re-enter your data because any data you entered will have been lost.

You sent your return, but you did not get a confirmation number. What should you do?

This means your submission was unsuccessful. You may have to reauthenticate yourself and re-enter your data because any data you entered will have been lost. To get help using My Business Account, go to Business enquiries.
